How to read a QQ plot

A QQ plot compares your data's shape to a normal. Points on the diagonal mean normal. The way the points bend tells you how the shape departs from it. Switch the underlying distribution and watch the signature change.

2The vocabulary

  • On the line. The data is close to normal.
  • Convex, bending up. Right-skewed: the high tail is longer than normal.
  • Concave, bending down. Left-skewed: the low tail is longer.
  • S-shaped, both ends peeling off. Heavy tails: extremes are more common than normal.
  • A flat step in the middle. More than one mode.

All of these are statements about shape. Notice what a QQ plot does not do: it will not tell you where a single value like 3 sits. That is a percentile, on a different page.
